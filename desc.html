About Me

As a kid not too far from an international port, my initial fancy was around the field of mechanics and large-scale automobiles. For instance, my hydraulic lift prototype in school won acclaim in a science-exposition. The benefits associated with that domain in the transformation of human work were innumerable. I knew about programming back then, but my viewpoint was limited to juxtaposing it with mechanical automations – as in, it also helps automate what humans do (just do and not think). Later in higher secondary school, I was introduced to machine learning, where machines were taught to observe, think, try, re-think, and then do. This broke all limits I thought I had set for machines in facilitating human living. In order to utilize the tremendous space for innovation this concept presents, my focus gravitated towards the world of computers. I started believing in the power of computer science and wanted to set my career in its direction.
