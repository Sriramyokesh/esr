About Me

As a kid not too far from an international port, my initial fancy was around the field of mechanics and large-scale automobiles. I spent time learning and modeling such systems. For instance, my hydraulic lift prototype in school won acclaim in a science-exposition. The benefits associated with that domain in the transformation of human work were innumerable. I knew about programming back then, but my viewpoint was limited to thinking of it as being similar to mechanical automations - that is, it also helps automate what humans do (just do and not think).

Later, I was introduced to machine learning, where machines were taught to observe, think, try, re-think, and then do. This broke all limits I thought I had set for machines in facilitating human living. In order to utilize the tremendous space for innovation this concept presents, my focus gravitated towards the world of computers. I started believing in the power of computer science and wanted to set my career in its direction.

I did my Bachelors' in Computer Science at SASTRA University, India and graduated with an honors in 2019. I then worked at Informatica for three years as an Research & Development Engineer before joining UIUC for Masters during Fall 2022.

I'm always interested in Problem Solving and you can find me thinking about strange algorithms for stranger problems.

I'm generally quite, love cricket and am passionate about the future of the world with the disruption coming through Aritifical Intelligence that humans develop.
